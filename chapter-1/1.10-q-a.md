Q: What is the difference between a statement and an expression?</br>
A: Expression is a non-empty sequence of literals, variables, operators, and functions that compute values. Statements are used when we want the program to perform an action.</br>

#2</br>
Q: Indicate whether each of the following lines are statements that do not contain expressions, statements that contain expressions, or are expression statements.</br>
a) ```int x;```</br>
b) ```int x = 5;```</br>
c) ```x = 5;```</br>
d) ```foo();```</br>
e) ```std::cout << x;```</br>

A:
a) statement that does not contain expression
b) statement that contains expression
c) expression statement
d) expression
e) expression statement (operator<< is a binary operator)
